• Night & Day

    LED Installation
    Unity VR Scene

    by Sumedh Vedanthi

    Artist Statement

    In the high-tech future, VR will allow people to see what the world was like before technology. This piece is a juxtaposition of nature and technology and simultaneously a convergence of the two to create two instances of the same scene. This intertwining of nature and technology exists not only in the scene but in most of our lives today. Technology is becoming integrated into nature and is even used to represent the latter. While the technological mediums of this piece (Led installation and VR) are diametrical in the type of environments they create, both are used to represent the natural world which is becoming more computerized everyday. Together they work harmoniously to present viewers both the night and the day; a world after technology and one before it respectively. When viewers step before the Night scene filled with LED trees, fiber-optic trees, and stars that are projected, they will see an artificial representation of nature within the realm of our natural reality. However, the moment they put on the VR headset to see the Day scene filled with organic forms of trees, mountains,a lake, and grass, they will see a realistic representation of nature within the virtual reality. Thus, the contradictory yet simultaneously co-dependent concepts of nature versus technology, real versus artificial, and night versus day are central to the piece in showing that these concepts are all two sides of the same coin, or in the case of this project, two phenomenon in the same scene.

    Process

    Night: To make the night scene, I needed regular trees for the foreground and pine trees in the background. For the pine trees I simply modified 8 fiber optic christmas trees by removing the stars on top and adding some artificial grass to the base of each of them. The three regular trees in the foreground were each made of thick copper electrical wire that I folded and pressed flat to make a 6in compressed loop of wire. I then soldered one end of the loop and cut the other end afterwards. I then bent out and put kinks in the end of the loop that I cut to make the branches of my trees. After that, I grabbed a thin enameled coperwire and scraped off the enamel on the end of it. I soldered the positive end of a 20mA LED to the thin copper wire and the negative end of that LED to the edge of each thick copper wire branch. Then I wrapped the thin wire all the way from the branch down to the trunk of the tree while wrapping some of the thin copper wire to other branches to give the tree some thickness. I repeated this step for each led on each tree that I made which was close to 40 LEDs total. After gathering all the thin copper wire at the base of the trunk, i wrapped them around each other and cut them evenly. I then added a resistor and attached my LED trees to usb cables so I could feed power directly into them through a battery pack. It was a daunting process to make sure the LEDs were properly soldered, didnt blow, or overheat. By the end of it, I had three working LED trees from the original four that were planned.

    The bases of the trees were then made of ply wood that was drilled and dremelled to fit the trees in the bases and allow for the ground and power wires to run from the bottom. After attaching the bases, I added some artificial grass to them, officially completing the night time of the scene.

    Day: To make the Day scene, I used Unity to build a vr landscape environment using packages that included a variety of mountain terrain stamp assets, a water simulation asset, various other terrain assets from rocks, grass, bushes, and most importantly the pine trees and regular trees that my main scene was comprised of. There were also the standard skybox, directional lighting, and simple camera rigs in additional to a VR camera rig that made everything visible and in the right perspective that I wanted the scene. First I tried molding the right kind of mountain arrangement I wanted using texture tools to push and pull the plane but it was looking distorted once I added the assets so instead I used a terrain stamp that fit closest to the scene I envisioned so I put it into my scene and from there added all the assets to make the scene complete and bring it to life. Finally after adding the textures for the mountains, trees, bushes, grass, and the reflection for the water, I added a player into the scene from whos perspective the person with headset would then view the scene. Adjusting the camera rig to the right location and adjusting the placement of the trees to fit the correct perspective of the night scene was a meticulous process. However even after all the detailing of the scene in Unity, there was another beast that I hadn't taken into account; operating the HTC Vive Headset. This involved setting up the lighthouses that the Vive uses to map the placement of you(the user of the headset) and your scene correctly. Then I had to load my unity package into a computer to run it through Steam VR. After it was in the Steam VR camera mode, making sure the scene was properly alligned to the camera rig I set up was an issue and at times the scene would dissappear or be at a wierd angle causing viewers to turn their head to see it level. It was daunting but overall and experience to learn and grow from.

    Bio

    Sumedh Vedanthi

    Sumedh is a Bay Area-based multi-media artist that persued and is recieving his BFA degree in Digital Media Arts at San Jose State University. In his time persuing his BFA he became exposed to, and even well-versed with a wide array of tools and software pertinent to web design, graphic design, film, photography, 3D Modeling, creative coding, robotics, augmented reality, virtual reality, and projection mapping. He hopes to use this arsenal of skills, tools, and software to accomplish his goal of stretching the boundaries of what a digital artist can be in the Silicon Valley, the tech capital of the world. The primary focus of his work is in graphics/logo/web/product design for personal brand development. As he immerses himself in the space of design, he wishes to further research and explore the advantages of good design for not only other artists, but also for entrepreneurs and innovators alike. He would also like to continue working with alternate mediums like animation and projection mapping to drive his artistic style and concepts across to viewers and participants of his work.
