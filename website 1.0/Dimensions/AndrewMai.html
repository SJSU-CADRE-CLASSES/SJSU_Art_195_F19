• Hidden Treasures

    by Andrew Mai

    Artist Statement

    Expansion on one's self as a form of art for appreciation of human form was what drove my piece forward. I entered a new medium using my digital art knowledge of 3D modeling and combining it with physical crafting. Hidden Treasures, is a suit of armor that can be worn, but is displayed above eye level to give the sense of a royal artifact. The inspiration came from the idea of design fiction and place what an artifact from a cyberpunk futuristic world would look like. The symbolism comes from myself as a black character because I tend to dress myself in all black while I am surrounded by colors. However, I treasure black as a color as it is the most dominant, which gives this piece as a sense of determination that was built into this. It holds a value of wants and needs that I deeply resonate in that space. Not everyone fufill the wants they want to pursue and use the needs as an excuse. I crafted the plinth as a hope for discussion between my audience in how they will achieve their wants for the new year.

    Process

    Armor piece: The armor was was first built in Maya and modeled for life size construction. After finalizing the model, it was then imported into a printable 3D model program called Pepakura. Pepakura is a program that unfolds 3D models into printable size piece. Instead of printing each paper out with printer paper, I decided to use our school resources and used the laser cutter. In doing so, I was able to use a much thicker material to make my piece for better construction.

    Everything was then spray painted black and used resin to harden the material even further. Sanded a little bit on the edges and firmly connect the pieces together. A blue tint film was then attached to the helment and armor holes to protect the face.

    Plinth and display glass: Instead of using wood, I used 5 ft particle board to construct the plinth as it was much cheaper. The display case had to be custom made from Tap Plastics of a 25in x 25in plexiglass. I drilled and used construction glue to hold the plinth. Chemical solution and sandpaper were used to mold the plexiglass together to form a display case.

    LEDs: I used IKEA LED lights with a fading program to place underneath the plexiglass and have the glass glow to the LEDs.
