    Truth of Blood Woods

    Video Game
    Godot Engine

    Artist Statement

    Truth of Blood Woods is a 2D platformer made throught the Godot game engine that aims to make the public aware how women are treated in gaming culture. As an avid video game enthusiasts I see woman players treated as though they are not people. Threats, toxic, language, and being taken advantage of, and other forms of dicomfort are what keeps gamig culture male dominant. Truth of Blood Woods puts you in the shoes of Sam, and explorer who discover a video game carnival that threatens the player for being a female.

    Construction

    Through the process of the open source game engine Godot I designed the layout of the game and constructed the level based off my concept. My artist Ariana Montoya helped fill in the role as a designer for the characters and background. Once the game was finished I proceeded to the physical part of the piece.

    I put a USB type c wired through a broken SNES system front to end connecting to a mac laptop. I connected a bluetooth SNES controller to the wire to give an aesthetic that you are playing through the system. I then borrowed an SNES game cartridge printing out a custom label of the game sticking it on the cartridge. After, I used a pedestal that was drilled on the wall with the laptop on top with as a sheet covering it with the SNES layed on top. Lastly I mounted a monitor display that would connect to the laptop projecting the game.

    About

    Diego Quiros

    Born in 1996

    Lives in San Jose, CA, USA

    Diego Quiros is someone who entered the digital world at a young age experiencing video games that made him question his place as an artist. Joining the Digital Media Art program in 2014 San Jose State ignited his passion for the digital era. Quiros has contributed to the digital arts from entering the Paseo Protyping Challenge 2019, SF Hackathon 2019, and presented multiple pieces in galleries shown at San Jose State. His passion for video game design, videography, and 3D modeling can be represented through his works. His other interests consist of photography, diverse coding and film.
