• Some Wandered in Desert Wastelands, Finding No Way To a City Where They Could Settle.

    Kris Cook. Video, CRT TV, Raspberry Pi, Electronics. 48 x 16 x 14 inches. November 2019.

    Artist Statement:


    I use a culmination of nostalgic aesthetics mixed with modern technologies to explore questions like how has our digital realm changed our current realm, have the technological accomplishments of the past brought us to a better future, and how can we create a better more technologically infused future that progresses our society. I manipulate scenes of a drying lake and vast deserts by distorting form and hues with a LZX video synthesizer, then I manipulate the 4k video into a signal that feeds through aged electronics to juxtapose past and present technologies. These scenes show desolate landscapes which show how much our technology has advanced and its relationship to the Earth and asks if it is worth it.

    About Kris Cook:


    Kris Cook is a digital media artist from the San Francisco Bay Area. She is an interdisciplinary artist who explores concepts of digital technology in society through photography, videography, motion graphics, and mixed media. Kris graduated from California State University, San Bernardino in 2012 with a BA in English Literature. She then studied at San Jose State University and graduated in 2019 with a BFA in Digital Media Art. She has exhibited her art in galleries throughout the Bay Area. Her work has been shown in galleries in San Jose, at the 2018 Maker's Faire in San Mateo, and at B4BEL4b in Oakland. Her film, The Next Step, won the Jury Award and the Audience Award from Campus Movie Fest in 2018. Kris was a Social Media and Website Development Manager with the SJSU Art and Art History Department and a Social Media Liaison with the SJSU Art Gallery. She currently works freelance and spends her time creating art works in her studio in San Jose. Visit HumanHybrids.io for updated information about Kris Cook.
